What Virginia Medicaid Covers

Virginia Medicaid (administered by the Department of Medical Assistance Services, DMAS) covers a broad range of services — many of which are directly relevant to managing PMOS/PCOS. Most members are enrolled in a managed care plan (Anthem HealthKeepers Plus, Aetna Better Health, Humana Healthy Horizons, Sentara Community Plan, or UnitedHealthcare Community Plan), and specific coverage details can vary by plan. The benefits below apply statewide, including the Richmond area.

Why this matters for PMOS/PCOS: Managing PMOS often involves specialist visits (endocrinology, OB/GYN), lab work, prescription medications (metformin, hormonal contraceptives, etc.), mental health support for anxiety and depression that commonly accompany PMOS, nutritional counseling for metabolic health, and sometimes fertility-related care. Virginia Medicaid covers all of these categories.
🩺

Specialist & Outpatient Visits

Covered — includes endocrinologist and OB/GYN visits, which are the primary specialist types for PMOS care. Referral may be required depending on your managed care plan.

Adult Preventive Services

Covered at no cost to members as of July 1, 2022 — for all adult Medicaid members. Relevant services include:

  • Adult wellness exam (annual)
  • Blood pressure & cholesterol screenings
  • Depression screenings
  • Nutritional counseling
  • Cancer screenings (mammography, etc.)
  • Recommended vaccines
  • Smoking cessation counseling

Behavioral Health

Covered — PMOS is strongly associated with anxiety and depression. Virginia Medicaid covers a broad range of mental health services including:

  • Outpatient therapy and counseling
  • Intensive outpatient programs (IOP)
  • Partial hospitalization (daytime)
  • Crisis services and mobile crisis teams
  • Community-based team supports

Plan First — Family Planning

Virginia's Plan First program covers birth control and family planning services — including for people who may not otherwise qualify for full Medicaid. This is especially relevant for PMOS, where hormonal contraceptives are a core treatment tool for regulating cycles and managing symptoms.

Plan First program details →
🏥

Inpatient Hospital Care

Covered — includes hospital stays when medically necessary. Virginia Medicaid covers both inpatient and outpatient hospital services.

📋

Lab Work & Diagnostics

Covered — lab tests ordered by your provider are covered. This includes hormone panels, blood glucose, lipid panels, and other labs central to diagnosing and monitoring PMOS. Imaging (ultrasound) is also covered when medically indicated.

How to find out what your specific plan covers: Once enrolled, you'll be assigned to or choose a managed care organization (MCO). Your MCO's member handbook is the definitive source for your specific coverage details. You can also call the member services number on your Medicaid card, or contact DMAS at 1-800-552-8627. To apply for Medicaid: commonhelp.virginia.gov or call Cover Virginia at 1-855-242-8282.
Important disclaimer: Insurance acceptance changes frequently, and the information here was compiled in May 2026 from publicly available sources. Always call any provider's office directly before scheduling to confirm they accept your specific Virginia Medicaid managed care plan. If you are on a Medicaid managed care plan (Anthem HealthKeepers Plus, Optima Health Community Care, etc.), the provider must be in-network with that specific plan — not just Medicaid generally. The Virginia Medicaid Find a Provider tool is the most authoritative source.
