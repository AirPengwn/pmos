Finding a PMOS/PCOS
Specialist in Richmond

A guide to endocrinologists and OB/GYNs in the greater Richmond area with expertise in Polycystic Ovary Syndrome — now officially renamed Polyendocrine Metabolic Ovarian Syndrome (PMOS) — who accept Virginia Medicaid.

Prepared for Erin Lamberton · May 2026
🔬 New Name Alert: As of May 12, 2026, PCOS has been officially renamed PMOS (Polyendocrine Metabolic Ovarian Syndrome) by a global coalition including the Endocrine Society. The old name is still widely used — search under both when looking for providers. The new name better reflects the hormonal and metabolic nature of the condition.

Before You Call

📋
Always verify Medicaid Insurance acceptance changes. Call any office before booking and confirm they take your specific Virginia Medicaid managed care plan (Anthem HealthKeepers Plus, Optima, etc.).
🔗
Use the DMAS finder Virginia Medicaid's official Find a Provider tool lets you search by specialty and your managed care plan.
💬
Mention the new name Ask about "PCOS or PMOS" experience. Not all offices will have updated their language yet — both terms refer to the same condition.
🚗
Transportation covered Virginia Medicaid covers rides to medical appointments when you lack another way to get there. See the transportation benefit page.
